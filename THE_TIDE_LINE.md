## THE COLD OPEN — The Ship of Separate Rooms *(Lance's canon, verbatim spirit)*

The family booked the crossing to get stronger. To get better. To connect again —
the trip that was supposed to fix everything by itself.

And on the ship, they did exactly what they did at home: **they moved to different
parts of the ship.** One at the bow. One in the cabin. One in the game room. One
on a phone in a stairwell. Same vessel, separate rooms — proximity wearing
connection's clothes. The prologue plays this quietly (two minutes, skippable,
seven cabin doors closing one by one, each with its own light under the door).

Then the storm. Nobody was together when it hit. That's the point. That's the
whole diagnosis, delivered by weather.

They wake scattered down one shoreline, with nothing — **on the island.** Our
island. The jungle where a boy once ran from a machine wearing his father's name
rises behind the beach. The sea the Wayward Crew sails lies ahead. The family
washes up on **the tide line between the two worlds** — the exact seam of the
connected universe. The mansion sits dark on its hill; a caretaker's lantern
still hangs at the old dock. Never explained. (The wink law.)

## THE LAW OF THE ISLAND

**Nothing important works when attempted alone.** Fires lit solo gutter out.
Shelters raised by one pair of hands lean. Water carried alone spills. The island
is not cruel — it is *honest*, the way the ship never made them be. It only
yields to together.

## THE ROBOTS — the Washed-Ashore *(Lance's direction: Ewok-cute, helping hands)*

The island's other residents: **little robots the sea brought in** — cousins of
the Wayward Crew, built for love in the same workshop lineage, shelved at almost,
lost in shipping. They live in **the Jumble**, a driftwood village in the rocks —
bucket-drums, tide-glass lanterns, doors too small for adults. Return-of-the-Jedi
energy: tiny, brave, resourceful, ceremonious about small things, instantly loyal
to castaways. They adopt the family on sight and help with every goal — carrying,
cheering, teaching by little plays. Honest-AI is structural: robots in fiction,
AI in fact; nobody ever pretends to be human.

The five (each embodies a family-systems pattern the family will recognize —
therapy by mirror, zero fingers pointed):
- **SKIP** — cannot stop chasing what he loves (the pursuer)
- **HOLLOW** — a shell-bot who seals shut under stress (the withdrawer)
- **ECHO-2** — repeats everyone slightly wrong (triangulation, made adorable)
- **BAILER** — bails water from dry boats (the anxious over-functioner)
- **THE COLLIER** — the old forge-keeper, half-buried, wakes when the forge is
  rebuilt (the elder; teaches mending; voice of the finale)

Skip and Hollow's chase-and-seal loop IS the pursue/withdraw cycle. In Season IV
the family maps it *for them* — and then the map looks familiar.

## THE ANTAGONIST — the Undertow

The family's own conflict cycle, externalized as weather-in-the-water. Never
voiced, never embodied, never defeated — **mapped, and learned to swim
together.** Nobody is the problem; the pattern is.

## THE FIVE NEEDS — connection is infrastructure

Warmth · Water · Food · Shelter · **TOGETHER.** The fifth decays like any meter
and refills ONLY conjointly (the fire circle, the shared meal, the story round,
the repair). Low TOGETHER makes everything harder; high TOGETHER is visible
everywhere — the fire burns taller, the camp glows, the Jumble drums at night.
No-death law: nobody starves or drowns; the camp never burns down; slips never
shrink built things.

## THE TWO RHYTHMS — the week and the gathering *(Lance: "tasks all week · family dinners · family meetings")*

- **THE WEEK** — each member, own phone, own castaway: real commitments mirrored
  to island work (appreciation texts, chores, kid one-on-ones, tool practice).
  Real work done = your castaway's work done — wood stacked, water drawn, **your
  lantern lit at the family dock.** Lanterns lit are celebrated; lanterns unlit
  are simply unlit. No shame mechanics, ever — the Undertow feeds on scorekeeping.
- **THE GATHERING** — family dinners and family meetings ARE the sync sessions:
  same room + one screen (pass-the-conch v1; the phone IS the conch), or each on
  their own device on the same map (v2 transport). The week's tending is waiting:
  the fire burns as tall as the week was tended. Milestones happen here.

## THE SEVEN — avatars for every family shape

Seven castaway slots (couples, families, blended, co-parents, three generations).
Each real person claims one at boarding; **unclaimed slots wake up as AI
castaways** (Washed-Ashore-adjacent — Lance's "AI plays characters not chosen").
Role tokens: Navigator · Keeper of the Fire · Lookout · Quartermaster ·
Storyteller · Anchor · Tender.

**THE PERSPECTIVE SWAP** (the crown; consent-first): play as each other — carry
their chores, answer the robots as them, tend what they tend. Swap banner always
honest, sessions bounded, debrief card after. The empty chair finally answers back.

## THE WAGE — planks and embers

- **PLANKS** — earned by real work and closed milestones; they physically stack
  at the camp and never un-earn (the wake law in wood). Milestone 27 is literally
  "Every Plank Has a Name."
- **EMBERS** — the small currency; feed the fire, gift the Jumble, light lanterns.
- **THE RAFT** (finale) is built from the season's own named planks — the reward
  economy IS the story's ending. By then you necessarily have enough. No grind
  wall, no spend-pain: the camp keeps everything it built.

## THE FINALE WINK *(connected-universe, restraint law: silhouette + one line, skippable)*

When the raft launches: if companion-paired, the lighthouse sweeps the water.
And far out, crossing the horizon — a ship with bone-white letters on the hull,
a small figure high in its crow's nest, holding a lantern up in salute. The crew
that chose the sea, keeping their promise: *somebody out there is waiting to be
found.* The family was the somebody. One frame. No dialogue. Gone.

*The last line of the season:* **"A family is not the boat that sank. It's what
they build from it."**
