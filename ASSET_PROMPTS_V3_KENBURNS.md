# ASSET PROMPTS v3 — THE 30 PICTURES THAT SLOW AND ZOOM
### Driftwood · the heartfelt transitions (Lance's 2026-07-12 commission: "at least 30 pictures that slow and zoom, for heart felt transitions throughout")

**Format for every image:** 1920×1080 landscape (the Ken Burns pan needs margin), painterly storybook realism, warm golden-amber palette against teal sea, soft rim light. **One shared style reference across the whole set** (the Foundry discipline). No text in image. Each entry lists WHERE it plays so wiring them is mechanical.

**The robots:** WOODEN — small, hand-sized to knee-height, built of weathered driftwood planks and branch-limbs, round knot-of-pine eyes, visible rope lashings and copper nails. Skip is the smallest — mismatched scraps, one hand wrapped in a palm-leaf bandage (after image 7). **Driftwood robots are wooden ONLY — the metal robots belong to the Rehabit "Voyager" app; never put a metal robot in a Driftwood still.**

## ⚠️ THE SILHOUETTE LAW (2026-07-12 inclusivity commission)
Driftwood serves every family shape — man+woman, two men, two women, families with kids, blended, all skin tones and mixes. The stills must never lock the player family into one look. So: **humans in shared story art are silhouettes, rear views, hands, or lantern-lit shapes — never fixed, readable faces, never a fixed number or gender.** The WOODEN ROBOTS carry all the close-up faces and all the personality; they're who the camera loves. This is not a limitation — a backlit family of shapes against the sea is *more* universal AND more cinematic. Where a prompt below still says "family of four" or names faces, read it through this law: **"family silhouettes, number and gender ambiguous, backlit / seen from behind."** Lance's face-less image library is the primary source; generate to match it.
